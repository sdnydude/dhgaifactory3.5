# ADHD CME Marketing Plan

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive marketing plan outlines a multi-channel strategy to promote attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) continuing medical education to psychiatrists and related healthcare professionals. With an $80,000 total investment, we project reaching 1,021 healthcare professionals through four integrated marketing channels.

### Strategy Overview
Our approach leverages trusted professional associations, credible medical publications, established CME networks, and conference partnerships to deliver targeted messaging about advancing ADHD clinical practice. The campaign emphasizes evidence-based strategies for complex patient presentations and diagnostic nuances.

### Key Performance Targets
- **Projected Registrations:** 1,021 participants
- **Cost per Registration:** $78.35
- **Campaign Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Target Audience Reach:** 45,000+ psychiatrists nationally

---

## Target Audience Profile

### Primary Audience: Psychiatrists
- **Practice Settings:** Private psychiatry practices, community mental health centers
- **Geographic Scope:** National
- **Estimated Universe:** 45,000 professionals

### Secondary Audiences

| Segment | Size | Rationale |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Child & Adolescent Psychiatrists | 8,500 | High ADHD caseload, subspecialty expertise needed for complex cases |
| Psychiatric Nurse Practitioners | 25,000 | Increasingly managing ADHD patients, need prescriptive authority education |
| Primary Care Physicians (Mental Health Focus) | 15,000 | Often first-line ADHD evaluators, need diagnostic and treatment guidance |

### Audience Insights

**Key Motivators:**
- New FDA-approved ADHD medications and treatment protocols
- Managing adult ADHD diagnosis and treatment complexities
- Addressing comorbid conditions (anxiety, depression, substance use)
- Board certification maintenance requirements
- Insurance coverage and prior authorization challenges

**Preferred Communication Channels:**
- American Psychiatric Association communications
- Psychiatric News and specialty journals
- Email from trusted CME providers
- Professional networks and peer recommendations
- Mobile-optimized online learning platforms

**Optimal Timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-8 AM, 12-1 PM, or 6-8 PM

### Content Guidelines
- Independent educational grant positioning
