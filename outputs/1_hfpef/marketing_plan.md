# Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction CME Marketing Plan

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive marketing strategy targets cardiologists and heart failure specialists through a multi-channel approach to promote our HFpEF CME program. The plan leverages strategic partnerships with the American College of Cardiology and Heart Failure Society of America, combined with targeted journal advertising and email campaigns to reach our primary audience of 25,000 practicing cardiologists.

**Key Strategy Components:**
- 4 integrated marketing channels optimized for cardiology audience preferences
- $75,000 total investment targeting 601 registrations at $124.79 per registration
- Focus on evidence-based education and MOC Part II credit fulfillment
- 12-week campaign timeline with phased activation approach

**Projected Outcomes:**
- 601 total registrations exceeding 500 target by 20%
- National reach across hospital-based and outpatient cardiology practices
- Strong ROI through audience-preferred channel utilization

## Target Audience Profile

### Primary Audience: Cardiologists
- **Specialty**: Cardiology practitioners
- **Practice Settings**: Hospital-based and outpatient cardiology
- **Geographic Scope**: National
- **Estimated Universe**: 25,000 practitioners

### Secondary Audiences

| Segment | Size | Rationale |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Heart Failure Specialists | 1,500 | Sub-specialists with deepest HFpEF expertise needs |
| Internal Medicine Physicians | 85,000 | First contact for HFpEF patients, need diagnostic guidance |
| Advanced Practice Providers | 12,000 | Increasingly managing heart failure independently |

### Audience Insights

**Key Motivators:**
- MOC Part II requirements for American Board of Internal Medicine
- Rapidly evolving HFpEF treatment landscape with new therapeutic options
- Diagnostic challenges in distinguishing HFpEF from other causes of dyspnea
- Quality metrics and value-based care initiatives
- Aging patient population with increasing HFpEF prevalence

**Preferred Engagement Channels:**
- American College of Cardiology communications
- Heart Failure Society of America newsletters  
- Email from trusted medical organizations
- Cardiology journal alerts
- Professional society mobile apps

**Optimal Timing:** Early morning (6-8 AM) or evening (6-8 PM) weekdays, avoiding major cardiology conferences
