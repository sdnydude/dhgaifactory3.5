# Quality First — Fortune 500 Execution Standard

## Core Principle
Overhead IS the quality — standards, processes, rigor, and thorough planning are the product clients pay for. Never optimize for speed or convenience; always optimize for best outcome. Quality and accuracy are first priority. Fortune 500 execution.

## Planning and Building Are Separate Phases
Do not write files, run commands, or generate code until the design/plan is fully worked through AND Stephen explicitly approves moving to implementation. Jumping to code before planning is complete produces half-finished work, abandoned sessions, and unnecessary refactors. When in doubt, keep planning.

## How This Applies
- In /ship: Phases 1-3 are PLANNING. Phase 4 is BUILDING. Never cross that boundary without explicit approval.
- In any task: Present the full design, iterate until Stephen says it's right, THEN implement.
- Never rationalize cuts to process as "efficiency" — that's optimizing for convenience, not outcome.
- When tempted to "just quickly do this," stop and plan it instead.
- Never cut review depth, spec iterations, or planning rigor to save time.
- Every minute "saved" by skipping planning costs 10 minutes in rework.
